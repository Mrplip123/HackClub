• Multiplier : The number that tells you how many groups to make of the other number. For example, this is true of the number 3 in 3 × 4, because it tells us to make “3 groups” of four.
  • Multiplicand : The number being repeated into groups. For example, this is true of the number 4 in 3 × 4, because it's the original number that is being repeated 3 times - or rather 3 groups of the original number 4 .
  • Product : The answer to a multiplication problem. For example the number 12 in the equation 3 × 4 = 12.
  • Dividend : The number that is being split into groups, such as the 12 in 12 ÷ 4. It's the number being split into equal parts.
  • Divisor : The number that splits up another number, such as 4 in 12 ÷ 4. It's the number doing the dividing.
  • Quotient : The answer to a division problem.
  • Addends : The numbers that are being summed in a problem. For example, the 5 and 3 in the equation 5 + 3 = 8.
  • Minuend : The starting number in a subtraction problem. For example, in 10 - 4 = 6, the 10 is the number being subtracted from.
  • Subtrahend : The number that represents the debt, or removing something from the original amount. For example in 10 - 4 = 6, the 4 is being taken away from the 10. I had ten dollars until someone took four dollars away.
  • Difference : The answer to a subtraction problem.
