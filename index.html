• Particle Theory : A theory that explains what matter is made of and how it behaves.
    • Heat : The transfer of energy from the particles of a warmer object to the particles of a colder object.
    • Kinetic Energy : Energy that all moving objects possess.
    • Thermal Energy : The total kinetic energy and energy of attraction in all particles of a substance.
    • Temperature : A measure of the average kinetic energy of particles in a substance.
    • Thermal Expansion : When solids, liquids, or gases are heated and their volume increases.
    • Thermal Contraction : When solids, liquids, or gases are cooled and their volume decreases.
    • Solid : A state of matter with a definite shape and definite volume.
    • Liquid : A state of matter with definite volume but takes the shape of its container.
    • Gas : A state of matter with no definite shape or volume.
    • Melting : The change of state from solid to liquid.
    • Freezing : The change of state from liquid to solid.
    • Evaporation : The change of state from liquid to gas.
    • Condensation : The change of state from gas to liquid.
    • Sublimation : The change of state directly from solid to gas.
    • Deposition : The change of state directly from gas to solid.
    • Particle : A tiny piece of matter that makes up all substances.
    • Average Kinetic Energy : The average energy of motion of particles in a substance.
    • Particle Motion : The constant movement of particles in matter.
    • Particle Attraction : The forces that pull particles toward each other.
    • Expansion Joint : A space in structures like bridges that allows materials to expand without cracking.
    • HVAC : Heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems used to control building temperature.
    • Central Heating : A system that heats a building using a furnace and distributes warm air or water.
    • Air Conditioning : A system that cools indoor air and removes heat.
    • Thermal Expansion of Solids : When solid materials increase in size when heated.
    • Thermal Expansion of Liquids : When liquids increase in volume when heated.
    • Thermal Expansion of Gases : When gases expand as temperature increases.
    • Density : The amount of mass in a given volume of a substance.
    • Volume : The amount of space an object occupies.
    • Mass : The amount of matter in an object.
    • Furnace : A heating system that burns fuel to produce heat.
    • Natural Gas : A fossil fuel often burned in furnaces for heating.
    • Petroleum : A fossil fuel used for energy and heating.
    • Kinetic Energy of Particles : Energy particles have because they are moving.
    • Particle Spacing : The amount of space between particles in matter.
    • Gas Pressure : The force created when gas particles collide with the walls of a container.
    • Environmental Impact : The effect human activities have on the environment.
    • Migration : The movement of animals from one region to another seasonally.
    • Panting : A cooling method used by animals like dogs to release heat.
    • Basking : When reptiles sit in sunlight to warm their bodies.
    • Hexagonal Ice Structure : The typical six-sided arrangement of particles in ice.
    • Balloon Expansion : A balloon growing larger when the gas inside is heated.
    • Tire Expansion : Air inside a tire expanding when it heats up.
    • Gasoline Expansion : Gasoline increasing in volume when temperature rises.
    • Ocean Thermal Expansion : The increase in ocean water volume due to warming temperatures.
