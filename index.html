• Statement: A sentence that shares information, an opinion, or a fact. Example: "The sky is blue" is one way to share a fact.
  • Department: A section of a larger organization where people work on similar tasks. Example: A hospital has a group of workers who focus only on helping children.
  • Entertainment: Something that people watch, listen to, or do for fun. Example: Playing video games or watching a movie is a way to have fun.
  • Announcement: Information shared with a group, usually in a public way. Example: A teacher might tell the class about an upcoming field trip.
  • Relationship: A connection between people, places, or things. Example: Two friends have a special bond because they care about each other.
  • Environment: The world around us, including air, water, land, and living things. Example: Trees, animals, and the weather are all part of the natural world.
  • Ownership: The right to have and control something. Example: A person who has a bike can decide when and where to ride it.
  • Instrument: An object used to create music or perform a task. Example: A piano makes sounds when its keys are pressed.
  • Experiment: A test done to discover something new or prove an idea. Example: Mixing different liquids to see what happens is a way to learn about science.
  • Treatment: The way something or someone is cared for or handled. Example: A doctor gives medicine to help a sick person feel better.
