  • sensible : Using good judgment and making smart, practical choices, such as choosing to wear a coat when it’s cold outside.
  • believable : Something that seems true or realistic, such as when a story a friend tells you sounds like it could have really happened.
  • favour : A kind or helpful act you do for someone else, often without expecting anything in return. Like when you help a friend with their homework just because you want to help them.
  • honour : A feeling of respect and admiration toward someone who has done something great. It can also mean to show respect for something or to adhere to a particular code of conduct, like standing up during the national anthem.
  • fountain : A structure that sprays water into the air, often found in parks or gardens. People sometimes toss coins into them and make a wish!
  • misinformation : So-called facts that are mistakenly false or incorrect. If someone accidentally shares the wrong facts, they’re spreading this around.
  • predisposition : A tendency to behave in a certain way or to like something more. For example, having a natural love of sports causes you to usually enjoy playing or watching a variety of sports.
  • compulsion : A strong urge to do something, even if you don’t really need to. For instance, having the impulse or unconscious need to check your phone often.
