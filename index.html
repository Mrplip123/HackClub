• Structure : Anything made of parts put together for a particular purpose.
  • Function : The task or purpose of a structure.
  • Form : The shape and physical appearance of a structure.
  • Gravity : A non-contact force of attraction that pulls objects with mass toward each other, noticeable when one object has a large mass.
  • Magnetic force : A non-contact force that pulls magnets together.
  • Electrostatic force : A non-contact force that causes static cling between charged objects.
  • Applied force : A force that occurs when objects come into contact through a push or pull.
  • Magnitude : The strength or size of a force.
  • Direction : The way in which a force acts, such as up, down, left, or right.
  • Mass : The amount of matter in an object, measured in grams or kilograms.
  • Weight : The force of gravity acting on an object, measured in newtons.
  • Load : A force acting on a structure.
  • Static load : A load caused by gravity that remains constant, such as the weight of a structure.
  • Solid structure : A structure that relies on solid materials and has a large mass to support loads.
  • Frame structure : A structure made of connected parts that form a skeleton to support loads.
  • Shell structure : A hollow, curved structure that is lightweight but strong and rigid.
  • Combination structure : A structure made from a combination of solid, frame, and shell forms.
  • External force : A force acting on a structure from outside the structure.
  • Internal force : A force acting between parts within the same object.
  • Point of application : The specific location on an object where a force is applied.
  • Plane of application : An imaginary flat plane through which an applied force passes.
  • Tension : An internal force that pulls particles in an object apart.
  • Compression : An internal force that squeezes particles in an object together.
  • Torsion : An internal force that twists an object.
  • Shear : An internal force that causes parts of an object to slide past each other, often leading to tearing.
